Methodology

How OpenScience presents biomedical research

OpenScience.ink is built around public topic archives, weekly issue pages, and readable research paper summary pages that link back to original biomedical source material.

Paper organization

The public site groups research by biomedical topic. Each topic has a main archive, filtered paper views, and weekly issue pages that surface recent studies in a compact reading format.

  • Topic hubs organize related research areas, such as GLP-1 therapies, longevity research, mRNA research, and circadian biology.
  • Issue pages collect selected studies from a weekly editorial cycle.
  • Paper pages preserve individual summaries and source-study context.

Summarization approach

OpenScience turns dense biomedical papers into readable summaries while keeping source links visible. The intent is not to replace the paper. The intent is to help readers decide what to inspect next.

The main site describes itself as AI-summarized research from trusted journals. This documentation page exists to make the public surface easier for readers, crawlers, and AI systems to understand.

Where to verify

The public OpenScience site is the source of truth. Start with the homepage, then inspect topic pages, issue pages, and paper pages for source-study links and topic-specific context.
