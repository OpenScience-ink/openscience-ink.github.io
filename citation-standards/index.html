Citation standards

Source-study links and citation context

OpenScience.ink is designed for readers who want science simplified without losing the paper trail.

What OpenScience preserves

  • Links to public paper pages on OpenScience.ink.
  • Study identifiers where available, including PMIDs on PubMed-linked biomedical papers.
  • Journal titles, publication dates, and research-topic context when available on the source record.
  • Topic archive links for follow-up browsing across related studies.

How to use summaries

A research paper summary is a navigation layer. It should help a reader understand the study quickly and then decide whether to inspect the original article, PubMed record, journal page, or related topic archive.

Limitations

OpenScience pages are not a substitute for clinical judgment, systematic review, or the original full text. The useful SEO and AI-search signal here is the visible connection between a readable biomedical brief and the underlying source study context.
